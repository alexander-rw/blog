# Growing up gaming

I owned a PS2 as my first real console when I was younger. My sister owned an Xbox, but I didn't play it as much as I played my PS2 (with the exception of Halo, and the [Buffy the vampire slayer game](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buffy_the_Vampire_Slayer_(2002_video_game)) which was an extremely fun one). 
I loved playing games. I eventually got a gameboy (advance SP), an Xbox 360 (and spent many nights playing burnout paradise and call of duty). I eventually moved onto PC gaming - A lot of Dota 2, and then recently (in the last few years), more single player games (Baldurs Gate, Outer Wilds, and many others). 
I met one of my closest friends gaming online, and although we don't play much nowadays, I'm still incredibly grateful that a game gave me a friendship that I never would have made otherwise.

A lot has changed in my life regarding this though. I tend to play significantly fewer games than I used to. I prefer in person games now - Board Game or Tabletop RPGs. The human connection aspect has become far more important to me than the game itself. I've found that I don't get as much joy out of multiplayer competitive or single player games as I've gotten older.
